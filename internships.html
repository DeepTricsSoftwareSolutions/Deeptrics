Internship Roles

Find your path. Each role works on real projects—what you practice depends on the team and project.

Frontend Developer

Build and improve user-facing interfaces.

You’ll practice: UI implementation, responsive layout, basic tooling.

Full Stack Developer

Work on both front-end and back-end of a product.

You’ll practice: APIs, databases, end-to-end features.

UI/UX Designer

Design flows, screens, and prototypes for real products.

You’ll practice: wireframing, prototyping, user-focused thinking.

Backend Developer

Work on servers, APIs, and data that power applications.

You’ll practice: APIs, databases, logic and structure.

Data Analyst / Engineer

Clean, analyze, and help use data for real decisions.

You’ll practice: SQL, spreadsheets, simple visualizations.

DevOps / Cloud

Help with deployment, automation, and running systems.

You’ll practice: CI/CD, containers, basic cloud usage.

Mobile Developer

Build or improve mobile apps for real users.

You’ll practice: mobile UI, platform basics, cross-platform or native.

What You Will Actually Do

This is how we’re different from certificate-only programs.

  • Work on real features, not demo tasks.
  • Use Git, issue tracking, and team workflows.
  • Attend real stand-ups and reviews.
  • Learn how products are actually built and shipped.

Duration & Commitment

We’re upfront so you can decide if this fits your schedule.

  • Duration: Typically 8–12 weeks, with some flexibility based on project and your availability.
  • Time commitment: A few hours to a part-time commitment per week—we’ll align with you during onboarding.
  • Remote / hybrid: Most work is remote; some teams may have optional sync sessions.
  • Final-year students: Depending on project and contribution, there may be opportunities to earn while working on real project work. We don’t promise this—we mention it so you know it’s a possibility.

Why This Is Free

DeepTrics funds internships through product development and client work. Students never pay fees. Ever.

Application Process

What happens after you apply—no surprises.

  • We review your application and get back to you within a few days to a couple of weeks.
  • Short screening may include a brief conversation to understand your goals and availability.
  • We care more about attitude and willingness to learn than prior experience. If you’re serious about building real skills, say so in your application.
